The project is conducted on the wine_quality dataset, which contains information about wines along with an indication of whether the wines are considered good (value 1) or bad (value -1). Due to limitations in the TabPFN model, specifically its large size (the model operates slowly on datasets with more than 1024 elements), the dataset has been restricted to a size of 2048 for the purpose of this task.

The dataset has been split into training and test sets in a 7:3 ratio. Four models were tested:

  • Logistic Regression
  • Gradient Boosting
  • Random Forest
  • TabPFN

They achieved the following results.

Accuracy:

  • Logistic Regression: 0.951
  • Gradient Boosting: 0.945
  • Random Forest: 0.943
  • TabPFN: 0.951

As seen, the achieved results are very similar and close to 1.

A noticeable difference is observed in the precision and recall values for different labels.

Precision(-1 vs 1):

  • Logistic Regression: 0.95 vs 0.00
  • Gradient Boosting: 0.96 vs 0.40
  • Random Forest: 0.96 vs 0.27
  • TabPFN: 0.95 vs 0.50

Recall(-1 vs 1):

  • Logistic Regression: 1.00 vs 0.00
  • Gradient Boosting: 0.98 vs 0.27
  • Random Forest: 0.99 vs 0.10
  • TabPFN: 1.00 vs 0.07
